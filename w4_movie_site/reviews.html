Review 12/02/2025

Qwamae Andrades

"Mid90s" is a poignant coming-of-age film that authentically captures the essence of adolescence in the 1990s skate culture. Jonah Hill's directorial debut is both heartfelt and raw, with Sunny Suljic delivering a standout performance as Stevie. The film's cinematography and soundtrack perfectly complement its nostalgic tone, immersing viewers in the era. While it explores themes of friendship, identity, and family dynamics, it does so with a genuine sensitivity that resonates deeply. Overall, "Mid90s" is a compelling and memorable film that offers a sincere look at youth and self-discovery.

Trivia

  • Jonah Hill drew from his own experiences growing up in Los Angeles to create an authentic portrayal of 1990s skate culture.
  • The film features real skateboarders in supporting roles, adding to its authenticity.
  • Mid90s was shot on 16mm film to give it a grainy, nostalgic look that reflects the era it portrays.
  • The soundtrack includes tracks from iconic 90s bands like Nirvana, Sonic Youth, and The Pharcyde, enhancing the film's atmosphere.
  • Sunny Suljic, who plays Stevie, had no prior acting experience before being cast in the film.
